ik. a digital identity Year: 2019 - 2020 | Duration: 6 months

Challenge

by law 575 services digitalised until 2022

by law usercentered services

In the year 2017 the german government passed the law called “Onlinezugangsgesetz” (OZG). It garanties the accessibility of govermentalservices in the internet. It also defines a usercentered expierence for german citizens to make them actually usable. By the year 2022 all 575 govermantal services should be digitalised.

This law was my motiviation to take a deeper look into govermental services from a designperpective, because the complexity and constrains make it very interesting how it could come together.

workflow to digitalize services

Every service from the ministeries gets standardized with the FIM principle. It translates the service into the right datatypes and fields to make it usable across all govermental plattforms. This services get then programmed and published by the ministery and can be used by all german federal states.

the industry

The Smart Country Convention gethers politicians and companies which work on the digitalization of govermantal infrasturctures. To get more insights on the current state and to understand the workflow of this industie, I visited the convention for three days.

Companies presented hard- and softwaresolutions. Politicians and minestrystaff presented the prograss they made on translating services and how they are making them userfriendly. As they said in interviews developing usercentered services is quiet a new thing for them. But the opinions in the talks split in two directions. On side wants to create transparency and self-government, on the other side data security is an important factor.

Ideation

touch points

If zoom out of conext we meight see two parties. One is the government which wants to provide all the services. On the other hand are the citizins which want to use this services. The overlapping space of this parties are the townhall or minsteries where citizens can take advantage of the services provided. For digital services the government needs a plattform to provide the services and the citizen needs a plattform for documents to share t hem with the government.

Share, manage and verify are the three most importent functions to to make use all govermental services. Combining them accelerates the whole process of administration for the citizens.

The expierence of the application should feel trustful and empathetic. Which means the documents are safe, well stored, but easiely accessible. Last but not least the users should identify with the app and the idea of self-goverment to use it on a regular basis.

story

With the age of 16 a german citizens start to take responsiblity for themself and others. This is a good startingpoint to get in conact with the app ik. As the first interaction with the system is to apply for the ID-card which every person needs have on them from the age of 16 on.

from story to app

This storyboard is the base of the initial design of the app. With that most of the goals and specifications metioned in the ideation can be combined in one process.

When opining the app for the first time the user can scan the personlaized QR-code for activation. After that a short welcome video welcomes the user and explains the functions.

Directly after the introduction the user can apply for the ID-card. This helps to fill the app directly with content and introduces the user to the workflow.

Sharing docuements should be as easy as possible. The big advantage of this app are the standartised services together with standartised documents. Therefore the system knows wich document ist needed and the user only has to give thier permission to share it. This reduces errors and accelerates the workflow on both sides.

After ther service the user gets to the home screen, where the latest statusupdates are presentet. Furthermore the user can see which documents are currently shared and for how long.

The interaction of sharing documents is in my opinion the most important one because it needs to be simple, but should be as clear as handing the document to someone in a real life situaiton. There for I started concentrating on that interaction the most.

in detail

The screen is divided in two main areas. The blue area is dedicated for official services. Yellow repesents the private area. For sharing a document the user has to move the document from “private” to “official”. This style is used through out the app. If the user needs to input personal data the background is yellow. For informations and request the blue background is used.
